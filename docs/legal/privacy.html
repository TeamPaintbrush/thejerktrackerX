The JERK Tracker X - Restaurant Order Tracking

Last Updated: October 24, 2025
Effective Date: October 24, 2025

Your Privacy Matters: The JERK Tracker X ("we," "our," or "us") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use our mobile application and web services.

1. Information We Collect

1.1 Information You Provide

We collect information that you voluntarily provide when using The JERK Tracker X:

  • Account Information: Name, email address, phone number, password (encrypted)
  • Restaurant Information: Restaurant name, address, business details
  • Order Information: Order numbers, customer names, order details, timestamps
  • Driver Information: Driver name, company affiliation (when checking in orders)
  • Profile Data: User preferences, role assignments, notification settings

1.2 Automatically Collected Information

When you use our app, we automatically collect certain information:

  • Device Information: Device type, operating system, unique device identifiers
  • Usage Data: App features accessed, time spent, interaction patterns
  • Location Data: GPS location (only when using geolocation features with your permission)
  • Log Data: IP address, browser type, access times, error logs
  • QR Code Scans: Timestamp and order ID when scanning QR codes

1.3 Permissions We Request

Our Android app may request the following permissions:

  • Camera: To scan QR codes for order tracking
  • Location: To provide location-based features (optional)
  • Notifications: To send order status updates
  • Storage: To cache data for offline functionality
  • Network Access: To sync data with our servers

2. How We Use Your Information

We use collected information for the following purposes:

2.1 Service Delivery

  • Create and manage your account
  • Process and track restaurant orders
  • Generate QR codes for order pickup
  • Enable driver check-in functionality
  • Provide real-time order status updates

2.2 Communication

  • Send order notifications and status updates
  • Respond to customer support inquiries
  • Send important service announcements
  • Provide technical support

2.3 Improvement & Analytics

  • Analyze usage patterns to improve our services
  • Develop new features based on user behavior
  • Monitor and fix technical issues
  • Enhance app performance and user experience

2.4 Security & Compliance

  • Prevent fraud and unauthorized access
  • Ensure compliance with legal obligations
  • Protect user safety and security

3. Data Storage & Security

3.1 Cloud Storage

Your data is securely stored using Amazon Web Services (AWS):

  • AWS DynamoDB: Order and user data storage
  • AWS Region: US East (N. Virginia) - us-east-1
  • Encryption: Data encrypted in transit (HTTPS/TLS) and at rest
  • Backup: Regular automated backups for data recovery

3.2 Local Storage

Some data is stored locally on your device:

  • Authentication tokens (encrypted)
  • User preferences and settings
  • Cached order data for offline access

3.3 Security Measures

  • Password Protection: Passwords hashed using bcrypt
  • Session Management: Secure JWT-based authentication
  • Data Encryption: TLS/SSL for all data transmission
  • Access Controls: Role-based permissions (Admin, Manager, Driver, Customer)
  • Regular Updates: Security patches and updates

4. Data Sharing & Disclosure

4.1 We Do NOT Sell Your Data

Important: We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information to third parties for marketing purposes.

4.2 Limited Sharing

We may share your information only in these circumstances:

  • Within Your Restaurant: Order data shared with authorized users (admins, managers, drivers)
  • Service Providers: AWS for hosting, email services for notifications (under strict data processing agreements)
  • Legal Requirements: When required by law, court order, or government request
  • Business Transfers: In case of merger, acquisition, or sale (users will be notified)
  • With Your Consent: Any other sharing will require your explicit permission
